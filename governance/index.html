Purpose

This Charter defines ownership, authority, and operational responsibility within Boleski Studios. Its primary objective is to ensure execution, accountability, and long-term studio solvency. Titles, access, and decision-making power exist solely to support output.

2. Operational Responsibility ("The Output Rule")

Non-Passivity Principle

There is no such thing as a passive Senior Partner.

Each Senior Partner must act as a Department Head (e.g., Development, Building, Art, Marketing, Operations).

Mandate

A Senior Partner is fully responsible for ensuring their department delivers results on time and to scope.

Execution vs. Delegation

  • A Partner may execute tasks personally or delegate them.
  • Delegation does not remove responsibility.
  • If delegation requires funding, the Partner is expected to finance it from their own resources or agreed departmental budget.

Example

If you are the Building Partner, you must either:

  • Build the map yourself, or
  • Pay and manage a contractor to build it.

Failure in delivery remains the Partner's responsibility regardless of method.

3. Access & Permissions

  • Studio Edit Access (Team Create) is granted only to Partners whose department requires direct technical interaction with game assets.
  • Access is a tool, not a perk, and may be revoked if no longer operationally necessary.

4. Governance: Board & Voting

The Board

The Board consists of:

  • The Studio Director
  • All Senior Partners

Board Authority

The Board votes on:

  • Studio roadmap and major pivots
  • Admission of new Partners
  • Demotion or removal of Partners for non-performance

Director Veto

The Studio Director retains final veto power on all Board decisions solely to protect studio solvency, legal safety, and long-term viability.

5. Associate Partner Rights & Limits

  • Voting Rights: None by default (advisory only, unless explicitly granted).
  • Operational Mandate: Optional. No department ownership unless promoted.
  • Access Rights: None by default.

Associate Partners hold equity but do not control execution or governance unless elevated to Senior Partner.

6. Equity Rules & Transactions

Equity as a Fixed Asset

  • Equity cannot be created freely.
  • All equity transfers must come from existing ownership.

New Partner Buy-In

To admit a new Partner:

  • Existing Partners must agree to dilute their shares, or
  • The new Partner must purchase equity directly from an existing Partner.

Any cash generated from a buy-in is distributed only to the Partners who relinquished equity.

7. Performance Failure & Forced Buy-Out

Dereliction of Duty

A Senior Partner may be subject to demotion or removal if they:

  • Fail to deliver agreed outputs for two consecutive milestones, or
  • Halt production for 30+ days without prior Board approval, or
  • Fail to finance or delegate required work within a reasonable timeframe.

Board Action

Upon majority Board vote:

  • The Partner may be demoted to Associate Partner, or
  • Removed entirely from the Partnership.

Valuation for Buy-Out

In the event of a forced buy-out, equity valuation will be calculated as:

The greater of:

  • The Partner's original buy-in price, or
  • A pro-rata share based on the average net studio earnings of the previous 6 months.

This valuation is final and non-negotiable.

8. Internal Rank Numbers (Clarification)

Rank numbers are internal identifiers aligned with Roblox permission systems and internal tooling. They do not imply hierarchy beyond what is defined in this Charter.

Closing Statement

Boleski Studios is built on execution, not symbolism. Ownership grants authority only when paired with responsibility. This Charter exists to ensure that every Partner contributes measurably to the studio's success.

Failure to execute is a choice. This structure ensures that choices have consequences.
